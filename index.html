01 — Past

Where I came
from

COMMUNITY

I grew up as a first-generation Cypriot with Belarusian roots. There were three points in my life that directed me towards industrial design which were my first job,a conversation with a solider and my comander as a role modal. During school I studied triple science. Nothing pointed me towards the direction of industrial design. Then in my senior year I started a part time job in a VR center for kids. That job taught me more than I expected. Not from the equipment, but from the people. Navigating overprotective parents, being patient with young children, and working with kids with disabilities taught me early that the people using a product matter just as much as the product itself. I didn't have the vocabulary for it then, but that was my first real experience of user empathy.

After school came 13 months of military service as a tanker. Long guard shifts gave me time I didn't ask for but needed, staring into nothing, thinking about where I was going. Two people shaped that thinking. A conversation with a senior soldier who had no plan as a young adult and regretting not having a five year plan, served as a warning that made me evaluate where I was going. And my commander, who led not through fear but through fairness and truth. Watching him made me understand what it meant to be a good leader, despite the strict hierarchy, he treated every person under him as an individual worth listening to. That's something I've carried into every team I've worked in since.

But what really changed me in the army was working on the tanks with my unit. Even with a language barrier the work became a form of communication. The labor of keeping something complex running built a community around it. I fell in love with making things work with my hands. That feeling of working on something bigger than yourself, alongside people who became more than colleagues, is what pointed me toward Industrial Design. Not a five-year plan, but a sence of community.

More than a Design. A Community.
02 — Professional Identity

Who I Am
as a designer

What is it like to work with me.

Growing up between two cultures without fully belonging to either could make you question your identity. I never did. Not because the gap wasn't there, it was, but because I always found belonging somewhere else. In school, in the army, in games. What I noticed early is that community doesn't come from shared background. It comes from shared experience.

The language barrier was with me long before the army. I have lived in Cyprus my entire life and still cannot speak the language fluently. Then I spent thirteen months where it was the only form of communication around me. That experience taught me something I have carried into every team since I can tell when someone's voice isn't being heard because mine often wasn't. It also taught me that actions communicate what words can't. How you show up, what you make, how you treat the work, that tells people who you are. I bring that into how I design and how I collaborate.

The other environment that shaped me more than I realized is video games. I loved them, the freedom you feel in a virtual world you can truly create anything, and I believe they have strongly influenced how I approach problem solving. But looking back I definitely see some side effects of my near addiction. I was completely consumed. Which affected me negatively, but now I have learnt from it, Whenever I approach a design I always think about how it can negatively affect a user, which was Main realization in CBL 1.

How do you design an experience that makes someone feel genuinely part of something? That question is where my design identity lives right now.

03 — Vision

What Design
can do

Immersion in video games.
Design can build community. But I want to develop a medium.

My vision centers on the untapped potential of videogames as tools for genuine education and personal development. The direction I want to take to approach this is through immersion, which isn't just about high-fidelity graphics; it requires both technical innovation and ethical engagement. It is about building systems that connect people to something real.

When players are genuinely immersed, whether it is due to the quality of the technology or the feelings that the game makes you feel. Created is space for development. I want to push the boundaries of how games bridge the physical and digital worlds to create this new form of development. By designing experiences that don’t just entertain, but actively shape how we communicate, learn, and grow.

I do not know yet where this leads. The goal for now is to develop the tools — technical, conceptual, collaborative — to follow the question wherever it goes.

04 — Present

What I am
learning

How have my expertise areas developed

Creativity & Aesthetics

To me, Creativity and Aesthetics is the core of what industrial design is. It isn't just about the final prototype or making something look "nice." It is the entire process of getting there. Because it is such a broad and subjective criteria, the experience can feel like a rollercoaster of constant ups and downs.

Creativity and Aesthetics is where I can really do what I love: working with my hands to make something tactile. However, when it comes to making something digitally, I struggle. This is why, for the majority of my second term, I have been focusing on learning coding and Fusion modeling to create visually appealing digital projects.

User & Society

Initially, I thought the 'User and Society' expertise area just meant taking the user's point of view into account during a product's development—mostly through questionnaires and interviews. Looking back, I had a limited understanding because I didn't have any prior formal education on these topics, which left a lot of room for development.

Through this study, I have learned it goes much deeper than I could have imagined. After working on CBL Project 1, I now understand that any product we create affects users both directly and indirectly. And that is a scary thought: when you create a product, you are in charge of how it is received by the general public.

This is best explained with an example. Take one of my favorite games of all time, Overwatch. It is a multiplayer first-person shooter where players choose heroes from different countries. Yes, it is a fictional story, but the countries are very real. Seeing how game mechanics could influence stereotypes in Project 1 made me relate this concept to something I know and love. I started looking into how game balancing could negatively affect how an entire culture is perceived, especially by younger teenagers.

User and Society has made me deeply aware of my influence on society's views. Moving forward, my goal is to be much better at identifying and avoiding biases in my products.

Technology & Realization

For me Technology and Realization is how well you can intigrate innovation in your projects. For Project 1 I had no experience in coding modaling or any technologies. But throught the second term I have found myself developing in coding languages such as puthon java and C++ through the variation in our courses. Additionally I have developed the first steps into technology by applying arduinos in my projects.

I have found that if i want to achieve my vision i have to improve my idea of technology to beable to immerse yourself in videogames it is not just the feeling you feel and how to improve those feeling my details. It is also improving the technology being used around the world.

Buisness & Entrepreneurship

I thought since I havn't had a buisness or worked at a making a company yet I wouldn't have any idea of how to make a running buisness. I would say that I was wrong, because even though I havn't had any formal lessons regarding buisness in project 1 we have already made some of the first steps. In project 1, the biggest complaint about our product was the size. Which made me start to think about the neuances of packaging how we can make it convinent for users. This is where I understood that Buisness and Entrepreneurship isnt just about the money it is making a lasting product.

Additionally to develop my understanding of this expertise area I am part of the next years Intro Weekend where I will have the role of treasurer I am incredibly excited to be part of this role because I believe it can feel a gap that i have in my expertise areas.

Math, Data & Computing

Math data and computing for me is how well you understand the data presented. How well you can manipulate it to get different idea's and extrapolate it into your design. The data you gather isnt always just numbers cuase you can change it into a solution. I believe gathering Data is one of the most important parts of a design process, since you base your iteration of that data. Wether that is qualitive data from international students or questionnaires rearding your project you develop your idea of the data behind the numbers.
